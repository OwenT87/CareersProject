Introduction

If you have ever been a high schooler, you know exactly how a lot of us feel right now, especially the classes of 2025 and 2026. This is the time when the most pressure is being put on us. Everyone is asking us what we want to do while we are still pretty much kids. The thing is, there are so many options out there, yet everyone seems to push college. Based on Inside Higher Ed, "86% of students feel pressure to pursue a four-year degree." However, according to a report by Hiring Lab, 52% of US jobs don't mention any formal education. This means there are many opportunities available without having to go to college if that's not your thing. I am not saying that college is a bad thing, but I am saying that it is not the only option. There are many other things you can do. You can go into the trades, join the military, or go straight into the workforce. The important thing is to find what is right for you. I am going to talk about a few of my options and what I think about them.

The first one is a construction manager. I have always been interested in construction and how things are built. I think it would be really cool to see something go from nothing to something. The second one is an electrician. I have always been interested in how electricity works and how it is used in our everyday lives. I think it would be really cool to work with electricity and see how it works. The last one is the military, specifically the Navy Seals. I have always been interested in the military and what they do. I think it would be really cool to serve my country and do something that not a lot of people can do.
