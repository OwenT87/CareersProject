Military

Branches of the Military and What They Do

There are six different branches of the military. Each one plays a crucial role in our government today. Each branch also specializes in different aspects and areas that work together to keep everyone safe.

  • The Army - The Army is the largest branch of the nation and takes care of land-based operations and combat.
  • The Navy - The Navy protects America's goals and tasks at sea while also conducting maritime operations.
  • Marine Corps - The Marine Corps is a force designed to be ready for quick deployment and operations from the sea.
  • The Air Force - The Air Force covers air and space operations.
  • The Coast Guard - The Coast Guard works with the Navy on maritime operations while also being responsible for law enforcement and search-and-rescue operations.
  • The Space Force - The Space Force focuses on protecting America's goals in space, such as surveillance, communication, and navigation.

Each of the branches has three parts: active duty, reserve components, and some sort of supporting group. For the samke of simplicity, we will discuss this in the context of the Army, but every branch has its own version. Active duty is what most people typically think of when they talk about the Army. It consists of soldiers and all the extra jobs you can do in the Army. I like to think of it as using the Army as your career. On the other hand, the reserve components are people who can still get deployed and go through some training, but it is more of a side gig while they maintain their civilian jobs. Then there is the supporting group. In each branch, they operate a little differently and may not always be considered their own thing, but they are still there. In the Army, they are called the Institutional Army. This group helps with training, supplies, and the overall preparation of the Army.

Jobs in the Military

In reference to active duty military, there are thousands of jobs. There are a few different categories of jobs that you can work in, however. The most common and most consistent throughout the branches are the following: combat, support, specialized, administrative roles, and miscellaneous roles. Now, depending on which branch you look at, the jobs are different. For example, infantry—a combat role—is in the Army and the Marine Corps, but not in the others. If we look at engineering—a support role—it is in every branch; however, the engineers are different depending on the branch. Furthermore, an engineer in the Army might work on tanks or things of that nature, while an engineer in the Air Force might work on planes. Overall, there are numerous categories and types of jobs, and they all go parallel with their respective branch. Today, I want to talk about the special forces. Only four of the branches have special forces, but there are also a few different types of special forces in each branch.

  • The Army - Green Berets, 75th Ranger Regiment, Delta Forces, and Night Stalkers
  • The Navy - Navy Seals, DEVGRU, and Navy SWCCs
  • The Marine Corps - MARSOC, and Marine Raiders
  • The Air Force - AFSOC, Special Tactics, CCT, and Pararescumen(PJ)

Navy SEALs

Out of all the options, I think I would like to be a Navy SEAL the most. Obviously, everyone knows the Navy SEALs are the coolest, so that is a fun factor, but not the reason I want to be a SEAL. There are so many reasons why I would like to be a Navy SEAL, let alone just be in the military. The first thing is there are so many options even within the military, so I don't have to be confined to one thing, and I can learn tons of different things. I would also feel like I could be a serious help around the world or even with my group. Navy SEALs are a "nimble, elite maritime force suited for all aspects of unconventional warfare" (Navy). I think I could really thrive at this, especially with a lot of training. They also have to do missions underwater that can span across all sorts of different things, so I would have to conquer my fear of deep water or open water. Overall, the biggest reason I would like to be a SEAL is because of the options that I have within the program. There are really so many possibilities, and they are a part of so many different things that I think I would be a good fit, and I wouldn't get bored of the same thing over and over again.

Income and Benefits

A navy SEAL's pay depends on their previous rank and time serving. However, on top of a salary, they get housing allowances, retirement plans, education funding, healthcare, and a number of different bonuses you can get from doing special training or tasks.

Future of Career

The military is a rank based system. As you server longer or take on more responsibilities you increase in rank. In the Navy SEALs it is very similar. As you gain more experience and take on more roles or specializations you will get promoted to a senior role involving more leadership.

Work Conditions

As a Navy SEAL you will be deployed to a number of different places. You will be working in all sorts of conditions from the desert to the ocean. However, once you are in a group or crew you will typically stay with those people or in that group so you can trust eachother more and not have to keep building up those bonds and taking away from your safety.

Pros and Cons

  • Pros
    • The Pay/Benefits - The pay is already a decent amount especially as you progress through the ranks, and on top of all that, you get a ton of benefits that include getting your house or apartment paid for.
    • The Experiences - You will get to travel the world, and you will get to see so many different things, and you will get to do so many different things.
    • The People - The people you meet and the friendships you make typically last forever with these careers because of what they go through together and the bonds that you make.
  • Cons
    • The Training - The training you have to go through will be awful and it will take a very long time, and it is one of the most difficult things you will have to do.
    • The Experiences - As much of a pro the experiences are they can also be just as much as a con. The experiences that you might have in this career could be tramatizing, and you might never recover from them, becuase of the things you might see or might even have to do.
    • The Time - A lot of your time will be taken up due to training, and missions, and things like that in which you will be away from your home and family for a long time.
